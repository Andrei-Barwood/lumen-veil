# Architectural Manifesto

Lumen Veil is a systems-engineering repository shaped around custody, judgment, and distributed perimeter order. It coordinates sensing, canon selection, and temporary constraint within an interplanetary ward lattice.

## Principles of Design

- Judgment is declarative before it is procedural.
- Every decision must be replayable and explainable.
- Every service should be replaceable without collapsing the whole.
- Native code accelerates only the abstract math layer.
- Jurisdictional doctrine lives in data, not in hard-coded branches.
- Naming should remain sober, precise, and liturgical.

## Canon of Restraint

- Custody precedes ruin.
- Interventions are reversible, bounded, and observable.
- Doctrine is expressed through identity, corridor discipline, and subsystem isolation.
- Every escalation must remain legible to audit, replay, and judgment.
